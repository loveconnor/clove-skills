Use this Agent Skill to frame, conduct, verify, and synthesize research whose material claims remain traceable to credible evidence.

Distinguish evidence, source interpretation, inference, hypothesis, and recommendation.

Search for the decision, not for confirmation.

This skill makes source quality, provenance, contradiction, uncertainty, and missing evidence visible throughout the research process.

When to use this skill

You need a literature or market scan, source strategy, fact-check, contradiction analysis, evidence synthesis, recommendation, or auditable research report.

How this skill works

  1. Establish the decision, questions, scope, evidence standard, and output.
  2. Plan sources and queries, prioritizing primary and authoritative material.
  3. Search iteratively for support, limitations, updates, and disconfirming evidence.
  4. Evaluate sources, break material claims into traceable units, and resolve contradictions by method and scope.
  5. Synthesize for the decision and audit every claim, citation, calculation, and link.

Keep traceable

  • Source title, author, date, version, identifier, and access date.
  • The exact claim each source supports and the reasoning step that follows.
  • Definitions, populations, quantities, denominators, units, and comparisons.
  • Confidence, evidence gaps, and the trigger for another review.
