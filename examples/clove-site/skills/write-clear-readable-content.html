Content & discovery skill

Write so people can use it.

Create accurate, plain, inclusive, accessible content and documentation that helps people find the point, understand it, and act correctly.

Optimize for relevance, findability, understanding, and usability.

Meaning comes before a tidy sentence.

Strong content makes the actor, action, conditions, evidence, and consequence clear at the point they matter.

Use it when

You are writing or revising UX copy, documentation, instructions, errors, policy, marketing, forms, labels, or conversational content.

Working method

  1. Define the reader, task, source of truth, access needs, and successful use.
  2. Model facts, actions, exceptions, conditions, and consequences.
  3. Outline for finding: title and main message first.
  4. Draft directly with precise, familiar language.
  5. Edit separately for meaning, structure, action, inclusion, and presentation.

Writing rules

  • Lead with the answer, status, decision, or required act.
  • Use descriptive headings and meaningful link text.
  • Keep conditions close to the action they govern.
  • Never invent testimonials, metrics, proof, or confidence.
